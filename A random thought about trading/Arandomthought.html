About VN30

VN30 is a stock index of the 30 largest market cap companies in VietNam (just like S%P500 in the US). The data was collected from 2009 to 2022 (around 10 years, 3284 days).

Research questions: How many days do we have to wait until there is an increase in the VN30 index?

(For example, today’s index decreased by 1% compared to yesterday, how is it likely to increase in 1 day, 2 days or 3 days or more? It’s just like Geometric distribution where a success is described as an increase and a failure is described as a decrease in this example).

1/ Getting started

Let’s have a look at this table

Change_rate is the rate of change in the index

Up_or_down: 1 is an up in the index and 0 is a down in the index

Looking at the first 3 rows, the index had increased for 2 days before it saw a drop (-1.42%) on the third day. This means we had waited for 3 days for a change in the index.

Similarly, the index changed every day for the following rows.

Sometimes we had to wait for 4 days for a change in the index.

What are the unique waiting days and their frequency?

their frequency: [734, 380, 220, 100, 60, 30, 17, 5, 4, 2, 1, 2]

And these are their proportions

Now you may notice it looks very similar to the Geometric distribution. We will shortly check that.

2/ Checking and hypothesis testing

If it does follow a Geom distribution, then the probability of an increase occurring = total number of increases / numbers of days = 54.02

Geometric distribution (p = 54.02%).

Here I plot them side by side

 

Since we want, we expect it to follow a Geom distribution, I would call the actual data is “observations” and our expected numbers are “expectations”

The next step is to do a hypothesis test.

If it does follow a Geom distribution, the observed waiting days and the expected waiting days are:

I binned the last 4 values to make it at least 5, in order for the chi square test condition to be met.

Now we can start calculating chi-square value and the p value

H0: The waiting days are independent of each other and they follow the geometric distribution.

HA: The waiting days are not independent of each other (there might be a pattern in the number of waiting days)

With the above data, we will find that the p-value is 0.197 which evidently supports us to reject the alternative hypothesis. There might be a pattern in the number of days we have to wait until there is an up.

3/ What if I narrow down 8 groups to 4?

Mathematically, narrowing down the number of groups means narrowing down the degrees of freedom which might result in a smaller p-value (since chi-square value is almost unchanged). Let’s try it out

The frequencies of observed positive days and the frequencies of expected positive days

The proportions of observed positive days and the proportions of expected positive days

Doing the hypothesis test again but with this data, our new p-value is 0.04. In other words, we have sufficient evidence to say that the price being up or down is independent of the previous days and  follows the Geometric distribution.

* Further question: narrowing down the number of groups and yielding a different result makes sense mathematically but what does it mean financially?

AFTERWORD

  • Saying whether tomorrow’s price goes up or down is almost an impossible task.
  • The waiting days (1, 2, 3 or more than 4) until there is a positive rate of change (in VN30 index) follows the Geometric distribution.
  • The waiting days (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 or more) until there is a positive rate of change (in VN30 index) are not independent of each other.
